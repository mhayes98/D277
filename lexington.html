The Heart of Bluegrass Country

Situated at the heart of Kentucky's lush inner Bluegrass region, Lexington stands as a vibrant suburban community, home to around 320,000 residents. Officially incorporated in 1831, the city boasts a storied history dating back nearly two centuries. Lexington's picturesque setting, surrounded by the gently rolling hills and verdant pastures of the Bluegrass landscape, has long captivated visitors and new residents alike.

Quiet Neighborhoods and Bustling Attractions

As the second-largest city in Kentucky, Lexington maintains a distinct suburban character, offering residents a high quality of life with quiet neighborhoods, abundant green spaces, and easy access to the natural splendor of the surrounding countryside. Yet the city also features a thriving urban core, home to a bustling business district, world-class healthcare facilities, and a vibrant arts and cultural scene. This harmonious blend of suburban tranquility and metropolitan energy gives Lexington a truly unique appeal.

Horse Capital of the World

Lexington's economy is anchored by its renowned status as the "Horse Capital of the World", with the equine industry contributing significantly. However, the city's economic portfolio extends beyond horses, with major employers like the University of Kentucky, a leading research institution. Lexington also boasts thriving healthcare, tech, and tourism sectors, positioning it as an economic powerhouse.
